🎯 Business Problem

Challenge: Telecommunications companies face significant revenue loss due to customer churn. Acquiring new customers costs 5-7x more than retaining existing ones.

Solution: Predictive model to identify at-risk customers before they churn, enabling targeted retention campaigns and reducing customer attrition rates.

Impact: Early identification of high-risk customers allows for proactive interventions, potentially saving millions in lost revenue and reducing customer acquisition costs.

📊 Dataset & Features

Data Overview

  • Total Records: 7,043 customer records
  • Target Variable: Churn (Yes/No) - whether customer left within last month
  • Feature Categories: Demographics, account information, services, billing details
  • Churn Rate: ~27% baseline churn rate

Key Feature Categories

Demographics

  • Gender
  • Senior Citizen status
  • Partner and Dependents

Account Information

  • Tenure (months)
  • Contract type
  • Payment method

Services

  • Phone service
  • Internet service
  • Streaming services
  • Tech support

Billing

  • Monthly charges
  • Total charges
  • Paperless billing

🔬 Methodology

1 Data Understanding & Setup: Loaded telco churn dataset, handled missing values and data type conversions
2 Advanced EDA: Univariate, bivariate, and multivariate analysis to identify churn patterns and feature relationships
3 Feature Engineering: Created new features, encoded categorical variables, and scaled numerical features
4 Model Training: Tested multiple algorithms including Logistic Regression, Random Forest, XGBoost, LightGBM, and CatBoost
5 Hyperparameter Optimization: Used RandomizedSearchCV for model tuning and performance improvement
6 Model Evaluation: Comprehensive evaluation using accuracy, precision, recall, F1-score, and ROC-AUC
7 Feature Importance: Analyzed top predictive features to provide business insights

📈 Key Findings

Top Churn Predictors

  • Contract Type: Month-to-month contracts have significantly higher churn rates
  • Tenure: New customers (< 12 months) are at highest risk
  • Monthly Charges: Higher charges correlate with increased churn probability
  • Tech Support: Customers without tech support are more likely to churn
  • Payment Method: Electronic check users show higher churn rates
  • Internet Service: Fiber optic customers have elevated churn risk

Business Insights

Contract Strategy

Incentivize customers to move from month-to-month to longer contracts with discounts or benefits.

Onboarding Focus

Implement robust onboarding programs for new customers to reduce early churn.

Value Proposition

Enhance perceived value for high-paying customers through premium services and support.

Support Investment

Promote tech support services as churn prevention tool, especially for high-risk segments.
